# Budget Plan Blog Style

Budget Plan publishes practical, privacy-first personal finance writing for people who want a calmer way to run their monthly money from their phone.

## Editorial Rules

- Write for European readers, but avoid country-specific legal or tax claims unless they are explicitly sourced.
- Do not invent statistics, survey results, customer stories, app store status, pricing, or product features.
- Do not offer personalised financial advice. Keep guidance educational and operational.
- Avoid hype, urgency, shame, and moralising language. The voice is clear, calm, and direct.
- Keep the product promise honest: local-first budgeting, no account requirement, no ads, and no data selling.
- Prefer simple examples with realistic monthly categories such as rent, groceries, transit, subscriptions, dining out, and savings.
- Every article should leave the reader with one concrete budgeting action they can take today.

## Structure

- Use one H1 only. It must match the `title` front matter.
- Start with a short introduction of two to three sentences.
- Use H2 sections for the main ideas.
- Use short paragraphs. Long blocks of text are not acceptable.
- End with a practical closing section, not a sales pitch.

## Screenshots (read with the `Images:` block in your instructions)

- Screenshots in shipped posts use files under `blog/assets/` as **WebP** (`.webp`), optimized for size.
- In Markdown use **only** site-root URLs: `![Short caption](/blog/assets/your-file.webp)`. Never use relative paths like `../assets/...`.
- **Weekly automation:** you will receive **one** assigned image URL and the image itself; the article must be **only** about that screen, with **exactly one** embed of that URL — no other `/blog/assets/` images.
- For manual posts not from the generator, still use only allowed URLs and keep screenshots meaningful; avoid cluttering with many images unless editorially necessary.

## Forbidden Output

- Placeholder text.
- Fake citations.
- Guaranteed savings claims.
- Investment recommendations.
- Medical, legal, tax, credit, or debt advice.
- Claims that Budget Plan syncs data, uses bank connections, or provides AI advice.
